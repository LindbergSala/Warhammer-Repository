# 11. NOTABLE FIGURES

The **Order of the Grail** is too young to possess a long roll of ancient masters, sainted dreadnoughts, and ten-thousand-year heroes. Its notable figures are instead first-generation anchors: warriors whose names have become inseparable from the Chapter’s transition from Greyshield formation to consecrated brotherhood.

They are not legends of a distant age.

They are the men who made the Order real.

## Aurelian Varron — Lord of the First Grail

Aurelian Varron is the first Chapter Master of the **Order of the Grail**, and the warrior most responsible for transforming a body of Sanguinius-line Greyshields into a true Chapter. He was raised from the command stock of the Unnumbered Sons after Baal, chosen not for warmth, eloquence, or political ease, but for judgement, endurance, and the terrible clarity with which he understood the Chapter’s purpose.

Varron wears **ancient Saturnine Terminator Armour**, a relic from the days of the Horus Heresy. The armour was gifted to the Order by the Blood Angels at the Chapter’s creation, not as decoration, but as burden. It was refitted under solemn supervision for Varron’s use and overpainted in the Order’s colours: very dark blood red plate, black panels, bone-white shrine markings, and antique gold relic trim. Its original provenance remains sealed within Baalite armoury records, though the Order’s Reclusiam teaches that the suit is a witness from the age when Sanguinius still walked among his sons.

His weapon is the **Concussion Hammer**, another Heresy-era relic granted alongside the armour. It is a massive brutal instrument, less elegant than the blades favoured by many sons of the Angel, but perfectly suited to Varron’s nature. In his hands it is a siege-breaker, warboss-killer, and judgement tool. The Order names it **Baal’s Pride**, though some Blood Angels armoury documents retain an older designation that has not been released to the Chapter’s full rolls.

Varron is brutal, devout, controlled, and utterly front-line. He speaks rarely, but every command carries the weight of sentence. He does not try to imitate the noble brightness of Baal’s oldest heroes. Instead, he embodies the Order’s darker truth: that new sons of Sanguinius must prove themselves by carrying ancient wounds without claiming ancient glory.

## Malthor Sevrayne — Reclusiarch of the Black Chalice

Malthor Sevrayne is the guardian of the Order’s soul and the officer most responsible for shaping its severe religious identity. He is a jump-pack Chaplain, a cold preacher, and a strict judge of vows. His black armour is marked with dark red Chapter symbols, bone scripture, red wax seals, and the skull-and-blood-drop badge of the Order.

Sevrayne stands at the point where Blood Angels inheritance and Black Templars influence meet. He does not permit the Order to become a copy of the Templars. He teaches that the Chapter’s faith must pass through **Sanguinius**, through Baal, through blood, sacrifice, mourning, and restraint.

## Ilyon Meres — High Sanguinary Priest of the Consecrated Vein

Ilyon Meres is the Chapter’s senior Apothecary and Sanguinary Priest equivalent, the keeper of its gene-seed, blood-records, and most dangerous truths. His armour is white and bone with dark red and black Chapter elements, marked by chalice-reliquaries, red gauntlets, and devotional script.

Meres is compassionate toward the Chapter’s brothers, but merciless toward corruption. He rejects the comfortable claim that Primaris creation has freed the sons of Sanguinius from the Angel’s curse. Official records describe the Order’s gene-seed as stable. Meres accepts this wording only as a bureaucratic category.

## Elyas Veyr — Chief Librarian, Keeper of the Veiled Grail

Elyas Veyr commands the Chapter’s Librarius, a cautious and closely watched brotherhood within the Order. His armour is dark blue-black rather than bright blue, bearing red Chapter markings, bone scripture, and sealed books of blood-oaths.

Veyr is controlled, sorrowful, and deeply wary of visions. His duty is not only to interpret prophetic dreams, Sanguinary omens, and warp-signs. It is to prevent the Chapter from mistaking every vision for command.

## Orran Kael — Forge-Warden of the Pilgrim Fleet

Orran Kael is the Order’s senior Techmarine and master of its strained armoury. He maintains the Chapter’s fleet, vehicles, Redemptor sarcophagi, relic systems, armour repairs, and the sacred-machine infrastructure of the **Chalice of Baal**.

Kael is pragmatic, suspicious of excessive relic-mysticism, and quietly indispensable. He also oversees the maintenance of Varron’s **Saturnine Terminator Armour** and the **Concussion Hammer**, though he does so under strict Blood Angels relic protocols.

## Cassiel Dravorn — First Captain of the Grail Knights

Cassiel Dravorn commands the **Grail Knights** and serves as the Chapter Master’s right hand during the Order’s most sacred assaults. He is noble, severe, protective, and violently honour-bound. His wargear varies by campaign need, but in all forms he wears a bone death-mask, black tabard, dark red armour, antique gold trim, and reliquary tokens of the Chapter’s dead.

## Hadriel Voss — First Master of the Grail

Brother-Champion Hadriel Voss is the first warrior to bear the title **Master of the Grail**. His office was created after the long Ork corridor wars beside the Black Templars, but deliberately shaped away from direct imitation of the Emperor’s Champion.

Voss was not chosen by vision. He was judged. The Chapter Master, Reclusiarch, High Sanguinary Priest, and senior officers named him the brother whose faith in the God-Emperor remained strongest, purest, and most unshakeable under blood, doubt, rage, and glory. The relic power spear gifted to the Order after Gathrog’s Belt became the weapon of his office.

## Vaelor Ardent — Grail-Knight of the Red Witness

Sergeant Vaelor Ardent is one of the most feared younger champions of the Order. A Baal-Witness and Grail Knight, he represents the generation of warriors for whom the Chapter’s religious identity is not a later consecration, but the only truth they have known. He wears a unique spiked death-mask helm gifted by Chapter Master Varron after his elevation among the Grail Knights.

## Malachor Rane — Captain of the Grey Unbent

Captain Malachor Rane is the Chapter’s most important internal critic. He is a veteran of the Greyshield years, loyal beyond question, but deeply concerned by the Order’s drift toward religious absolutism. He believes zeal can become strategically blind.

Rane remembers the Unnumbered Sons as they were: temporary, practical, brutal, obedient, and clear. He argues that the Order must never forget those lessons. A world may need to be abandoned. A shrine may need to be destroyed. A relic may need to be left behind. A brother may need to die unrecovered so that a campaign can still be won.

For this, younger zealots mistrust him.

For the same reason, Varron keeps him close.
