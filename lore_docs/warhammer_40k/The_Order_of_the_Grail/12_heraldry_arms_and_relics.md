# 12. HERALDRY, ARMS & RELICS

The heraldry of the **Order of the Grail** is deliberately severe.

It is not the bright splendour of an ancient angelic court, nor the clean heraldry of a Chapter secure in ten thousand years of inheritance. It is the armour of a young brotherhood trying to prove that new-made warriors may still carry sacred blood. Its colours are dark, votive, and funereal: red for Sanguinius’ blood, black for vow and judgement, bone-white for death accepted, and antique gold for relics not yet old enough to be trusted without burden.

The Chapter’s livery is meant to look like a crusade shrine marching through ash.

Its warriors wear **very dark blood red or oxblood red armour**, usually muted rather than bright. The Chapter’s secondary colour is **black**, most commonly used on shoulder pads, weapon panels, tabards, kneepads, jump packs, shields, vehicle doors, turret casings, and selected helmets. The Chapter’s trim is **antique gold or dark brass**, deliberately worn, tarnished, and shrine-like rather than parade-bright.

Bone is used for veteran helms, death masks, command helmets, scripture panels, tabards, skull devices, honour markings, and reliquary casings. It represents the dead as witnesses.

## Chapter Badge

The Chapter badge of the Order is a **simple white skull with a single red blood drop upon the forehead**.

It is most often displayed on the **left pauldron**, set upon a black field. The skull is plain and unadorned. The blood drop is small but vivid.

The skull represents death accepted in the Emperor’s service. The blood drop represents Sanguinius’ sacred bloodline, Baal’s sacrifice, and the belief that martyrdom may become victory when offered correctly.

The plainness of the emblem reflects one of the Chapter’s earliest vows:

**No ornament can improve upon sacrifice.**

## Pauldrons and Battlefield Identification

The **left pauldron** is normally black and bears the Chapter badge. The **right pauldron** is also black and carries company, squad, battlefield role, crusade, or campaign markings. The pauldron trim is antique gold or dark brass for most line warriors.

## Helmet Colours

**Line brethren** wear dark red helmets matching their armour.

**Sergeants** wear dark red helmets marked by a black stripe, bone faceplate, or small bone command device.

**Veterans** wear bone-white or ivory helmets.

**Grail Knights** wear bone-white death masks or ivory helms.

**Command officers** often wear bone-white helmets with black or antique gold details.

**Chaplains** wear black armour and skull helms, marked with red Chapter symbols and bone scripture.

**Librarians** wear dark blue-black armour rather than bright blue, with red Chapter shoulder panels and bone script.

**Apothecaries and Sanguinary Priests** wear white or bone armour with red and black Chapter elements, red gauntlets, and blood-drop chalice details.

**Techmarines** use Mechanicus red panels integrated into the Chapter’s dark red and black scheme, often with brass shrine-work and machine-script.

## Company, Squad and Battlefield Role Markings

Company markings are displayed on the right kneepad, right pauldron, tilt shield, lower greave, or small heraldic plate. They usually use crusader-style numerals, blood drops, chalice marks, bone pips, black-and-bone campaign symbols, or small shrine-glyphs.

The Order uses battlefield role markings, but they are stylised as sacred signs:

- **Battleline:** white or bone tactical arrow upon black.
- **Close Support:** crossed blade, assault sigil, or descending blood-drop blade in bone.
- **Fire Support:** chevron, heavy support device, or shrine-like ammunition glyph in bone.
- **Veterans:** skull, chalice, laurel, or blood-drop honour mark in bone.
- **Command:** small grail, crown, blood-drop halo, or relic plate.

## Robes, Tabards, Seals and Scripture

The Order’s robes and tabards are usually **parchment-white, dirty bone, ash-stained ivory, or black**. Line veterans and honour formations often wear bone tabards marked with oath-text and blood-script. Senior religious officers may wear black tabards as signs of judgement.

Purity seals are common, but the Order refers to many of them as **oath seals**. Their wax is deep red, shaped at times like blood drops, chalices, or skulls.

## Vehicle and Fleet Markings

The Chapter’s vehicles are painted primarily in **dark blood red**, with black panels, black turrets or doors, antique gold aquilas, and bone-white campaign markings. Large Chapter badges are placed on doors, glacis plates, turret shields, aircraft wings, and Repulsor hull panels.

Fleet markings use black prows, red hull bands, bone-white ship icons, and votive scripture along launch bays.

## Greyshield Honour Marks

Veterans of the Unnumbered Sons may carry a narrow grey stripe beneath the left pauldron rim, inside a tilt shield, along the inner greave, or beneath a purity parchment. This hidden mark is called the **Pilgrim Mark**. It honours the **Pilgrimage of Unnumbered Blood**.

The Pilgrim Mark is never allowed to replace the Chapter badge.

## Armageddon Campaign Marks

Prepared Armageddon marks include an ash stripe across one knee or greave, blackened lower armour, small hive-glyphs on the right knee or pauldron, white Ork-kill notches scratched into black panels, and temporary Operation Imperator badges on vehicles, banners, or right kneepads.

No permanent Armageddon honour has yet been authorised. The Chapter has not earned it.

## Young Relics and Sacred Arms

The Order’s relic culture is young, but intense. Most of its relics are not ancient heirlooms. They are damaged armour plates from Baal’s aftermath, recovered fragments from Leviathan-cleansed shrine-moons, blood-sealed weapons from the Ork corridor wars, Black Templars-gifted honours, gene-vault tokens, broken banners, and ash canisters from worlds the Chapter could not save.

The greatest relics in the Chapter’s possession are the wargear of its Chapter Master: the **ancient Saturnine Terminator Armour** and the **Concussion Hammer** known within the Order as **Baal’s Pride**.

Both are relics from the days of the **Horus Heresy**. Both were granted to the Chapter by the **Blood Angels** at its creation. Their transfer remains one of the strongest visible signs that Baal did not regard the Order merely as Cawl-made reinforcement stock, but as sons of Sanguinius entrusted with inheritance.

The Saturnine armour is kept under layered authority: Chapter command, Armoury maintenance, Reclusiam rite, Apothecarion witness, and sealed Blood Angels provenance. Its plates are overpainted in the Order’s dark red, black, bone-white, and antique gold colours, but certain original marks are preserved beneath removable shrine plates.

The Concussion Hammer, **Baal’s Pride**, is massive, brutal, and old. It lacks the elegance often associated with the sons of the Angel, but that is part of its significance. In Varron’s hands it declares that the Order will not be only beautiful, swift, or noble. It will also be the hammer at the gate, the judgement at the breach, and the weight that breaks the enemy’s advance.

## Heraldic Taboos

The Order refuses symbols implying that Primaris warriors have replaced the older sons of Sanguinius. It avoids excessive Cawl iconography, bright angelic gold as main identity, crude Ork trophy display, and any symbol that implies the Chapter belongs more to Mars than to Baal, Terra, and the Emperor.

The red says they are sons of Sanguinius.  
The black says they are bound by vow.  
The bone says the dead are watching.  
The gold says inheritance is rare and dangerous.  
The grey, hidden beneath armour, says they were once Unnumbered.  
The skull and blood drop say their lives are already forfeit, but not meaningless.
