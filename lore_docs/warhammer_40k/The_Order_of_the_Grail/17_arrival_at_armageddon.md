# 17. ARRIVAL AT ARMAGEDDON

The call to **Armageddon** reached the **Chalice of Baal** through fractured command channels, carried by astropathic relay, crusade cipher, and reinforcement order rather than by a single clean voice.

Some within the Chapter later claimed that the plea bore the weight of prophecy. Captain Malachor Rane rejected that interpretation. Forge-Warden Orran Kael recorded only the practical truth: the fleet was scarred, under-repaired, and still low on several classes of munitions after the Ork corridor wars, yet remained closer, faster, and better suited to response than many heavier Imperial formations.

Reclusiarch Malthor Sevrayne gave the matter its final shape before the assembled Grail Companies.

Armageddon was not calling for saviours.

It was calling for witnesses who could still strike.

The Order accepted attachment to **Operation Imperator** without protest. The assignment was not punishment, nor exile, nor ceremonial honour. It was strategic use. The Chapter had fought Tyranid aftermath wars and knew the cost of arriving too late. It had fought Orks in hard corridor campaigns and understood the danger of momentum. It had operated beside the Black Templars long enough to understand crusader warfare, and carried Blood Angels lineage strongly enough to stand beside the sons of Baal without requiring explanation.

The wider Imperial command required mobile counterassault forces able to reinforce collapsing positions, secure orbital drop zones, break ash-waste spearheads, and restore morale in sectors where mortal defenders were beginning to fail.

The Order of the Grail was made for such war.

It also carried every danger such war could awaken.

## The Translation to the War World

Before the fleet translated toward Armageddon, the Chapter performed the **Counting Before Armageddon**.

The dead of Baal were named.  
The lost of Saint Kalix were named.  
The burned and quarantined of Vharis Tertius were named.  
The vanished aboard the *Mercy of Illyr* were named.  
The unfinished victims of Warboss Ghorzakh Irontread were named.

Then the Chaplains spoke the names of those not yet known.

Hive defenders.  
Ash-waste convoy crews.  
Shrine militia.  
Astra Militarum regiments.  
Serfs.  
Pilgrims.  
Unrecovered Astartes.  
Citizens who would die before the Order reached them.

Each entry was sealed in black ink and red wax.

**Name unknown. Number unknown. Witness accepted.**

Only after this rite did the fleet translate.

## First Strategic Assignment

Operation Imperator command assigned the Order of the Grail to a collapsing hive-front where ash-waste Ork armour columns were pushing through refinery roads toward a sacred Imperial district and its surrounding manufactorum belt.

The theatre was already unstable. Ork Speedwaaagh! outriders had overrun several convoy routes. Deathskulls looters had stripped wrecked Imperial armour and marked captured vehicles with stolen shrine icons. Goff assault mobs were gathering behind the armoured spearheads, ready to turn breach into massacre. Mek-led columns had established mobile armour nests among burned refinery structures.

Reclusiarch Sevrayne named it a site of witness before the first drop. Captain Rane warned that such naming should not become a chain around theatre command. Varron listened to both.

Then he gave the order to deploy.

## The Order’s Initial War Role

The Chapter’s first Armageddon deployment was divided into oath-cadres.

**Phobos cadres** descended first, their armour muted dark red and matte black, with gold dulled beneath ash pigment. Their mission was to identify Ork armour routes, mark warboss banners, locate Mek generators, and guide later drop forces through polluted crosswinds and anti-air fire.

**Tacticus formations** followed to secure orbital drop zones, refinery road junctions, and evacuation corridors. Their task was not glory. Their task was to create ground solid enough for the Chapter’s heavier vows.

**Gravis spearheads**, including elements of the **Red Sepulchre Brethren**, were committed against Ork-held manufactoria and Mek-built armour nests.

The **Ash-Vowed** were placed along hive gates, shrine roads, refinery bridges, and evacuation routes.

The **Grail Knights** remained in reserve until the first confirmed appearance of major Ork command elements.

The **Master of the Grail**, Brother-Champion **Hadriel Voss**, was assigned to the warlord-hunt cadre.

## Allies and Necessary Friction

The Order’s first Armageddon operations required coordination with multiple Imperial bodies: Astra Militarum commanders, Imperial Navy officers, Adeptus Mechanicus personnel, Ecclesiarchy representatives, Blood Angels and successor elements, and Black Templars forces where present.

The Order did not resent scrutiny.

It had come to be judged.

## The Ork Threat Before Them

The Orks facing the Order were not a single clean host. They were pressure.

Evil Sunz outriders cut through ash routes in storms of speed and smoke. Goff mobs gathered behind crude armour wedges, waiting for breaches to widen. Deathskulls looters dragged wreckage, icons, fuel tanks, and shrine-metal into obscene new war machines. Mek bosses fortified refinery ruins with scrap shields, gun towers, and unstable power fields.

This form of war was familiar to the Order. That familiarity made them dangerous. It also made them vulnerable.

Reports of **Ghorzakh Irontread** began to spread before the Chapter’s first full cycle of deployment was complete: a warboss glyph seen on a captured tanker, a Mek-engine pattern matching the corridor wars, a shrine statue bolted to the prow of a battlewagon, a convoy massacre marked by iron-tread symbols.

None of it was proof alone.

Together, it was enough to darken the Chapter’s mood.

## What Armageddon Must Prove

To Imperial command, the Order of the Grail must prove its operational value.

It must break Ork momentum.  
It must reinforce collapsing lines.  
It must secure orbital and ash-waste routes.  
It must coordinate with older Chapters without demanding symbolic precedence.  
It must spend its assault strength wisely.  
It must not become trapped by every sacred site that cries for aid.

To itself, the test is simpler and more terrible.

The Order must prove that it can arrive before the line breaks.

It must prove that it can remember the dead without joining them too eagerly.

It must prove that the sons of Sanguinius born from Cawl’s vaults can carry the Angel’s wound, not as replacement, not as imitation, and not as delusion, but as duty.

## First Contact at the Shrine Road

The Chapter’s first major commitment on Armageddon came along a shrine road leading from the outer refinery districts toward the lower approaches of the hive-front.

The road had already been broken in three places. Guard armour burned in the ash. Pilgrim barricades had been crushed beneath Ork wheels. A shrine convoy lay scattered across the approach, its reliquary vehicles looted and dragged into crude barricades. Mortal defenders still held the final gate, but their vox traffic had become fragmentary.

The Order translated from orbit in layered assault.

Phobos beacons marked the descent.  
Tacticus squads secured the broken road.  
Red Sepulchre Brethren burned Ork nests from refinery shells.  
Ash-Vowed locked shields at the gate.  
Grail Knights descended when the first Ork boss pushed through the smoke.

Varron did not lead the first strike.

He waited until the second breach began.

When the Orks brought forward a looted engine bearing Imperial shrine metal across its prow, the Chapter Master entered the field in Saturnine plate. **Baal’s Pride** struck the engine’s front assembly and broke it inward with a thunderous force recorded across three Guard vox channels. The Order’s banners advanced behind him, black and red against the ash.

The shrine road held.

It was a small victory by the scale of Armageddon.

The Order recorded it anyway.

Not as triumph.

As arrival.

## ARMAGEDDON DEPLOYMENT ORDER: INITIAL COMMITMENT

Ash-Vowed detachments may be used to anchor mortal withdrawal lines, but must be monitored for overcommitment. Red Sepulchre Brethren authorised for manufactorum and armour-nest clearance where infrastructure loss is acceptable. Chapter Master Varron to coordinate with Operation Imperator theatre command and allied Adeptus Astartes forces as required.*  
>   
> — Operation Imperator Deployment Order, Armageddon War-Zone Allocation

The **Order of the Grail** has come to Armageddon as one Chapter among many.

It does not command the war.  
It does not own the legend of the world.  
It does not replace the heroes, martyrs, regiments, Chapters, and common defenders whose blood has already soaked Armageddon’s ash.

It has arrived with a younger burden.

From Cawl’s vaults, it received form.  
From Guilliman’s crusade, it received motion.  
From Baal, it received grief.  
From the Blood Angels, it received inheritance.  
From the Black Templars, it received the black chain of public faith.  
From Leviathan, it learned aftermath.  
From the Orks, it learned momentum.  
From Armageddon, it now receives judgement.

The Chapter’s banners stand at the hive approaches, still unproven in this war. Its ash stripes are fresh. Its oaths are newly sealed. Its dead are counted, but its Armageddon dead have not yet been named. The Grail Knights wait beneath black wings. The Ash-Vowed stand before gates that may not hold. The Red Sepulchre Brethren move through manufactorum smoke like funeral engines. The Master of the Grail listens for the name of a warboss. Chapter Master Aurelian Varron walks in Heresy iron, carrying a hammer given as trust and warning alike.

The Order of the Grail came to Armageddon to break Ork momentum before it became another devastation.

Whether it will be remembered as shield, blade, relic, warning, or ash remains unwritten.
