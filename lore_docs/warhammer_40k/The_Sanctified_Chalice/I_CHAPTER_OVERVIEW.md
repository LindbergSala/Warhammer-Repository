## I. CHAPTER OVERVIEW

### The Sanctified Chalice

**Archive Classification:** Ultima Founding Chapter Dossier
**Founding Status:** Ultima Founding
**Chapter Type:** Primaris-only Adeptus Astartes Chapter
**Parent Gene-line:** Blood Angels
**Recognised Lineage:** Sons of Sanguinius
**Known Battle-Cry:** *“By His Throne, by His Blood.”*
**Secondary Litany:** *“In Blood We Are Sanctified.”*

The **Sanctified Chalice** are a Primaris-only Blood Angels successor Chapter raised from the gene-seed of Sanguinius during the desperate years of the Indomitus Crusade. Their first warriors were not born into an ancient fortress-monastery, nor instructed beneath the banners of ten thousand years of Chapter glory, but marched first as Greyshields among the Unnumbered Sons, bearing temporary heraldry through a galaxy split open by the Great Rift. Only after war, loss, crusade service, and bloodshed in the shadow of Baal were they recognised as a distinct Chapter, their existence sealed not by ancient tradition, but by necessity, sacrifice, and the approval of those who judged the sons of Sanguinius too precious to waste and too dangerous to leave unnamed.

They are a Chapter of young oaths and old blood.

The Sanctified Chalice do not possess the accumulated relic-hoards, ancestral sagas, or long-buried dead of ancient Chapters. Their earliest saints are still within living memory. Their first banners still bear the smoke of their first true campaigns. Their oldest honours are not marble-walled legends from the Age of the Imperium, but scarred crusade-roll entries, blood-stained oath parchments, sealed casualty tallies, and the fragmentary testimony of worlds that survived only because red-armoured angels descended through fire.

Their identity is severe, devotional, and still hardening.

They worship the Emperor of Mankind openly as the **God-Emperor**, absolute sovereign of the species and divine master of the Throne. They venerate **Sanguinius** not merely as gene-sire, but as the greatest saint of mankind: the winged martyr, the holy blood, the perfect sacrifice, the son who died so that the species might endure. To the Sanctified Chalice, the inheritance of Sanguinius is not only genetic. It is sacramental. Blood is proof. Blood is debt. Blood is judgement.

Their name was not chosen lightly.

The **Chalice** is the vessel of holy blood: the blood of martyrs, the blood of gene-line, the blood spilled in the Emperor’s service, and the blood that must never be wasted on cowardice, corruption, or faithless hesitation. To be **Sanctified** is not to be pure by birth. It is to be made worthy through suffering. This distinction matters greatly to the Chapter, for they know they were engineered in vaults, awakened by command, and deployed as a weapon before they had a soul of their own.

They have spent their brief existence trying to prove that a weapon may become holy.

Their early reputation within crusade records is one of **ritualised shock assault**, close-range fire discipline, and pitiless boarding warfare. They favour the advance under fire, the oath-bound charge, and the violent seizure of sacred or strategic ground. Intercessor formations hold the line beneath black pauldrons and dark oxblood armour, while Assault Intercessors and jump-pack brethren deliver the killing stroke with the fury expected of Sanguinius’ blood. Bladeguard Veterans, still few in number, serve as relic-guardians and living icons of the Chapter’s forming cult of martyrdom. Their Chaplains, Judiciars, and Sanguinary Priests possess unusual influence for so young a Chapter, acting not only as spiritual guides, but as judges, confessors, executioners, and keepers of the Chapter’s fragile identity.

They are already respected for their courage.

They are already feared for what that courage costs.

In battle, the Sanctified Chalice have shown a willingness to bleed themselves white rather than yield oath-marked ground. They are protective of Imperial faithful with a severity that borders on possessive zeal, yet merciless toward heretics, xenos, oathbreakers, and those Imperial commanders who abandon the helpless without sanctioned cause. Several crusade observers have noted that the Chapter’s mercy is real, but narrow. They will die to save a shrine-hab of starving civilians. They will burn the next city to ash if it shelters the servants of the Archenemy. Their compassion and cruelty are not contradictions in their own doctrine. Both are offerings.

Their relationship to the Indomitus Crusade is direct and defining: **the Sanctified Chalice exist because the Indomitus Crusade required new angels of war to hold together an Imperium that was bleeding faster than it could be saved.**

They were born from Guilliman’s authority, Cawl’s gene-craft, and the mass deployment of the Primaris Space Marines. Yet they do not speak of such origins with ease. To older Imperial institutions, they are evidence of salvation and blasphemy bound in the same flesh. To some Blood Angels successors, they are younger brothers forged too quickly, taught too much by hypno-vault and crusade necessity, and burdened with a flaw they did not yet understand when first sent to war. To the Mechanicus, they are successful gene-stock and strategic yield. To the Ecclesiarchy, they are increasingly useful, increasingly fervent, and therefore increasingly watched. To the Black Templars who fought beside them, they are not copies, not pupils, but blood-sworn crusade brothers whose faith was hammered into shape amid void war, distress signals, and the screams of the dying.

The Chapter’s colours are already recognisable across several crusade-front registries. Their armour is a deep **oxblood red**, so dark that in low light it appears almost black until struck by flame or muzzle-flare. Their shoulder pads are **black**, their trim **antique gold**, their veteran helms **ivory or bone white**, and their tabards the colour of old parchment. Their weapons are generally black-cased and marked with red wax seals, devotional scripture, and kill-oaths written in careful High Gothic script. Their Chapter badge is a **simple white skull marked by a single red blood drop upon the brow**, a symbol plain enough to be painted quickly upon war-plate, coffins, reliquaries, and the doors of condemned sanctuaries.

It is a young symbol.

It has already been found carved into the walls of dead ships.

Administratum marginalia from the early crusade period record conflicting impressions of the Chapter. Some reports praise them as exemplars of Primaris discipline fused with angelic wrath. Others describe them as dangerously susceptible to martyrdom cults, overzealous Chaplain authority, and an accelerating reverence for blood-relics unsuited to so newly founded a Chapter. One Ordo-affiliated notation, sealed and later partially redacted, describes the Sanctified Chalice as *“a blade still wet from the gene-vault, already praying over its own edge.”*

The Chapter’s own annals reject the accusation of instability.

They claim only that the age is unstable.

They claim that the Great Rift has turned the stars into an open wound, that the Imperium is a cathedral on fire, and that a warrior who does not understand sacrifice has no right to stand before the Throne. In their developing catechisms, the Fall of Cadia is remembered as the shattering of the gate, the Devastation of Baal as the revelation of holy suffering, and the Indomitus Crusade as the long procession of mankind through darkness beneath the gaze of a wounded God.

> **Devotional Fragment, attributed to the early Reclusiam of the Sanctified Chalice**
> *The Emperor is God upon the Throne.*
> *Sanguinius is His Blood made sorrowful.*
> *We are the cup raised beneath the wound.*
> *Let nothing spilled in faith be lost.*

The Sanctified Chalice are not an ancient brotherhood reborn.

They are not the restored glory of a forgotten lineage.

They are a new Chapter, made in haste, armed in desperation, judged by suspicious allies, watched by old powers, and driven into war before their own traditions had cooled from the forge. Yet from that harsh beginning they have formed a clear and terrible purpose: to become worthy of the blood they carry, to make worship from warfare, and to prove that even a Chapter born from vaults, commands, and emergency decree may become sacred through sacrifice.

In the present records of the Era Indomitus, they stand as a grim product of their age: beautiful, fanatical, politically burdened, and already stained by the wars that made them necessary.

They are the chalice held beneath a bleeding Imperium.

They are the sons of Sanguinius who learned from crusaders that faith may be used as a weapon.

They are the Sanctified Chalice.

And they have only begun to fill themselves with blood.
